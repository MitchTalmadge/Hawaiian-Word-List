puaʻa kai
certain fish (as āhole, ʻamaʻama, ʻanae, humuhumunukunukuāpuaʻa, kūmū, pualu) used as substitute for pig offering. lit., sea pig.
puaʻa kau

puaʻa kumulau
sow, pig who has borne young.
puaʻa kāne

puaʻa laho
boar; term of reproach for a lecherous male, named for the pig demigod, Kamapuaʻa. lit., scrotum hog.
puaʻa lau
name for plants that might replace pig in some sacrifices, a favorite being young taro leaves. Others were ʻamaʻu, hāpuʻu, kūkaepuaʻa, kukui, olomea, and ʻuhaloa. They were considered the plant forms of the pig demigod, Kamapuaʻa. lit., leaf pig.
puaʻa māiki
small pig.
puaʻa nui huelo huluhulu
horse (old name, Lānaʻi). lit., big animal hairy tail.
puaʻa ohi
a young female pig that has not borne young.
puaʻa olomea₁
a striped pig.
puaʻa olomea₂
poetic name for the olomea, a tree. Kamapuaʻa took this form when pursued by Pele.
puaʻa pipi
early name for cattle when brought to the islands by Vancouver. lit., beef (Eng.) pig.
puaʻa pūkoʻa
spotted pig. lit., coral head pig.
puaʻa wahine

puaʻa wāwae loloa

puaʻa ūkō
pig offering at the death of a chief as a plea to the ʻaumākua gods to receive the spirit of the departed. lit., fulfillment pig.
puaʻa ʻehuʻehu
red pig, a poetic name for ʻāmaʻumaʻu, ferns. The demigod Kamapuaʻa took this form when he ran from Pele to the forest of ʻamaʻu ferns and turned into a fern himself.
